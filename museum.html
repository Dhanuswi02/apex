Museums in India

India is home to a rich variety of museums, ranging from historical and cultural institutions to art and science museums. Notable ones include the National Museum in Delhi, the Indian Museum in Kolkata, and the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya in Mumbai.

Explore Museums in India

National Museum,New Delhi

The National Museum in New Delhi is one of the largest museums in India, showcasing a vast collection of artifacts spanning thousands of years of Indian history. Its exhibits include sculptures, paintings, manuscripts, textiles, and archaeological finds, offering insights into India's rich cultural and historical heritage.

Indian Museum,Kolkata

The Indian Museum in Kolkata, established in 1814, is the oldest and largest museum in India. It houses a diverse collection of artifacts, including ancient sculptures, paintings, fossils, and Egyptian mummies, as well as exhibits on art, archaeology, and anthropology, providing a comprehensive view of India's cultural and natural history.

Salar Jung Museum, Hyderabad

The Salar Jung Museum in Hyderabad is one of India's most renowned museums, famous for its impressive collection of art, antiques, and manuscripts. It showcases over 43,000 art pieces, including sculptures, textiles, and rare artifacts from various cultures, with notable works like the Veiled Rebecca and the Double Musician sculpture.

Victoria Memorial Hall,Kolkata

The Victoria Memorial Hall in Kolkata is a grand historical museum and monument dedicated to Queen Victoria, built in the early 20th century. It houses a vast collection of colonial-era artifacts, paintings, sculptures, and manuscripts, offering a glimpse into the British colonial period in India. The memorial is set amidst lush gardens and is an iconic landmark of the city.
